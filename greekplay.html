The Seven Plays in English by Sophocles

Author: Sophocles

OED.

Prince, thou art come to my desire. Some God hath happily directed this thy way.

THE.

What is befallen? Son of Laius, tell!

OED.

My path slopes downward, and before my death I would confirm to Athens and to thee my promised boon.

_Oedipus pauses for a moment, looking towards the heavens._

THE.

What sign dost thou perceive that proves thine end so near?

OED.

The Gods themselves with herald voices are proclaiming it, nought failing of the fore-appointed signs.

THE.

What are these tokens, aged monarch, say?

OED.

The loud continual thunder, and the darts that flash in volleys from the unconquered hand.

_Thunder sounds loudly in the distance._
